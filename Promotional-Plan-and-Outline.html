Promotional Plan and Outline

home page advertisement

Objective

Engage and captivate an audience interested in the forthcoming personal experiences of Grade 10-AM students at APEC Schools Tondo during the 2023-2024 school year. The focus is on building anticipation and excitement as students prepare to share their stories through interviews, exploring the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on their social skills.

Target Audience:

  • Educational researchers
  • Educators and school administrators
  • Students, parents, and anyone interested in knowing the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on education and social skills.

Chanels and Tactics

I. Pre-Interview Phase
Teaser Campaign
        ● Launch a teaser campaign on social media platforms (Instagram, Facebook, Twitter) with visually appealing graphics and captions announcing the upcoming interviews. 
        ● Use anticipation-building language to create curiosity.

Behind the Scenes Content
        ● Share behind-the-scenes content, such as preparations for the interviews, student reactions, and snippets of the interview process. 
        ● Use Instagram Stories, Facebook Live, or short video clips on TikTok to give a glimpse into the making of the interviews.

II. Personalized Story Teasers
Social Media Sneak Peeks
         ● Release brief video teasers featuring Grade 10-AM students sharing their expectations for the interviews and thoughts on the challenges they anticipate discussing. 
        ● Maintain a consistent posting schedule to keep the audience engaged.

Student Spotlight Posts
        ● Create additional individual social media posts or blog snippets highlighting specific Grade 10-AM students. 
        ● Showcase their expectations and reasons for participating in the interviews using visually engaging graphics and impactful quotes.

III. Exclusive Story Previews
Exclusive Blog Previews
        ● Continue the weekly release of exclusive blog post previews on the school's website or social media platforms.
        ● Integrate expectations and reflections from students as they prepare for the interviews. 
        ● Ensure visually appealing content.

Podcast Episode Trailers
        ● Develop short podcast episode trailers that incorporate snippets from previous teasers and students' expectations for the upcoming interviews. 
        ● Share these trailers on social media platforms and podcast platforms.

IV. Virtual Student Panel:
Live Student Panel Announcement
        ● Announce the live virtual panel discussion with Grade 10-AM students as a culmination of the pre-interview phase.
        ● Use captivating language and visuals to generate excitement and emphasize the upcoming interviews.

Interactive Q&A Session Promotion
        ● Encourage the audience to submit questions for the student panel and express their expectations for the upcoming interviews. 
        ● Utilize countdown posts and engaging graphics on social media to enhance participation.

Timeline

I.  Pre-launch (2 weeks before the first story release)
        ● Initiate the teaser campaign on Instagram, Facebook, and Twitter.
        ● Share behind-the-scenes content across various platforms.
        ● Announce the upcoming exclusive blog series and podcast episodes with visually appealing graphics.

II. Exclusive Story Previews (weekly releases)
        ● Release exclusive blog post previews and podcast episode trailers on the school's website and social media platforms.
        ● Include expectations and reflections from students.

III. Virtual Student Panel (mid-campaign)
        ● Host the live student panel discussion with Grade 10-AM students.
        ● Utilize social media platforms for live streaming, allowing a broader audience to participate.
        ● Promote the event with countdown posts and engaging graphics, emphasizing the upcoming interviews.

IV. Post-Virtual Student Panel (transition to Interview Phase)
        ● Use the live panel to officially announce the start of the interview phase.
        ● Encourage the audience to submit more questions and share expectations for the upcoming interviews.
        ● Share highlights and audience reactions from the live panel.

V. Continued Engagement (throughout the campaign)
        ● Share additional behind-the-scenes content as the interviews progress.
        ● Promote upcoming exclusive releases and events through regular updates on all platforms.

VI. Post-campaign (after all stories are released)
        ● Compile all stories into a visually appealing digital collection or video montage.
        ● Encourage sharing and discussion of the complete series.
        ● Express gratitude to the participants and the audience for their engagement.

Evaluation: 

  • Educational researchers
  • Educators and school administrators
  • Students, parents, and anyone interested in knowing the impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on education and social skills.
